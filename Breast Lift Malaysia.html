What Is a Breast Lift?

A breast lift, or mastopexy, is a surgical procedure designed to raise and reshape sagging breasts. Major medical institutions such as the Mayo Clinic describe breast lift surgery as a method to raise and reshape breasts when changes from aging, pregnancy, or weight fluctuations cause sagging.

It involves removing excess stretched skin, repositioning breast tissue to a higher position, and elevating the nipple and areola to a natural level.

Nipple Elevation

Repositions downward-pointing nipples to a youthful level.

Skin Reduction

Removes excess stretched skin envelope for firmness.

Why Breasts Sag (Ptosis)

Breasts naturally change over time due to various factors. Sagging is common, normal, and treatable.

Pregnancy & Nursing

Volume changes stretch the skin.

Weight Fluctuations

Loss of fat volume causes deflation.

Aging & Gravity

Loss of skin elasticity over time.

Genetics

Inherited tissue quality.

Common Concerns Addressed:

  • Breasts that sit lower on the chest
  • Loss of firmness and projection
  • Stretched or enlarged areolas
  • Breast asymmetry due to sagging

Types of Breast Lift Offered

The best technique depends on the degree of sagging, breast size, and skin quality.

1

Periareolar Lift

Donut Lift

Best for mild sagging. Incision around the areola. Can reduce areola size. Minimal scarring pattern.

2

Vertical Lift

Lollipop Lift

Best for moderate sagging. Incision around areola plus a vertical line to crease. Stronger reshaping and projection.

3

Anchor Lift

Inverted-T

Best for significant sagging or heavier breasts. Greatest lift and skin removal. Excellent for major reshaping.

4

Lift + Implants

Augmentation Mastopexy

For lifted breasts plus more volume. Enhances upper pole fullness. Best when volume loss is a major concern.

Post-Operative Recovery

Immediate / Week 1

Support bra applied. Mild soreness. Return to desk work (comfort dependent). Avoid lifting.

Week 2–3

Swelling reduces. Bruising improves. Light walking encouraged. Support bra continues.

Week 4–6

Resume light exercise (with approval). Shape appears more settled. Scars begin to mature.

Month 3–6

Final contour becomes more natural. Scars continue to fade. Full shape refinement.
